Blockchain lexicon

Terms Definitions
Bitcoin It is by far the most well-known digital currency. It announced blockchain technology to the world. It has a feature whereby new bitcoins are generated and added to the system, having an inflationary effect. It’s distributed structure of the system coupled with its cryptographic functionality make it incredibly robust. It’s blockchain uses resource-intensive algorithms.
Block Bundle of new recorded transactions which is added as the latest link on a long 'chain' of historic transactions forming a blockchain.
Blockchain Decentralised, encrypted and self-executing technology enabling the recording of lists of transactions. Transactions are only accepted if all the users agree that it is legitimate. Its best-known use is for digital currencies such as Bitcoin. By embedding code in the blockchain, transactions can be executed automatically in response to certain conditions being met, providing a 'guarantee of execution’. Self-executing smart contracts based upon this functionality are developing rapidly.

Hashing Feature of the blockchain technology. It is a process through which a document is transformed into a fixed length code which is described as a digital fingerprint.
Ledger In working progress…
Middleman Intermediary which is trusted by all users, who has total control over the system and is responsible for mediating every transaction on a ledger (for example banks, governments, etc.).
Mining Process in which the transaction is packaged into a 'block' of transactions, and added, to the ever-growing list of blocks that form the blockchain ledger.
Nakamoto, Satoshi It is the pseudonym for the mysterious and elusive publisher(s) of an article describing how cryptography, combined with a distributed public ledger, could be used to implement a digital currency without a central authority to authenticate payments. His article is the fundamental on which Satoshi Nakamoto launched the Bitcoin.
Ownership history Record of digital property which enables the blockchain to protect consumers and creators of digital works of all kinds. It enables customers to ensure that they were buying legitimate goods rather than illegitimate copies, and could also enable rights holders to enforce their rights.
Peer-to-peer In working progress…
Permissioned blockchain-based voting Electoral authority system, maintaining control over the distribution of pseudonyms to guarantee anonymity and affirming their role as the ultimate authority and central mediator through which all votes must pass.
Pseudonymity Feature of the blockchain technology. The fact that all transactions are transparent, but they are not explicitly connected to real-world individuals or organisations, shielding the identity of parties along the supply chain without compromising the integrity of the record.
Scarcity In working progress…
Smart contract Computerised transaction protocol that executes the terms of a contract. Self-executing transactions in response to certain conditions being met, providing a 'guarantee of execution'.
Token Certificate of identity for unique digital goods that can be transferred with the blockchain technology.
